4
5 A "decoder" is a closure `(binary -> {String.t(), [{non_neg_integer(), binary()}]})`
6 that maps raw font-code bytes to UTF-8 text plus a list of unresolved sentinels.

8 ## Simple fonts (Type1, TrueType, etc.)
9
10 Cascade per byte (delegates to `Pdf.Reader.Encoding.resolve_byte/3`):
11 ToUnicode CMap → /Differences + AGL → base encoding → U+FFFD + sentinel.
12
13 ## Composite fonts (Type0/Identity-H/V)
14
15 When `/Encoding` is `Identity-H` or `Identity-V`, the font is dispatched to
16 `Pdf.Reader.CID.Decoder.build/2`. The CID decoder consumes bytes in 2-byte
17 big-endian chunks and resolves via:
18 ToUnicode CMap → Adobe registry table (Japan1/CNS1/Korea1/GB1) → U+FFFD.
19
20 Non-Identity predefined CMaps (`UniJIS-UTF16-H`, `GBK-EUC-H`, etc.) are
21 also supported when bundled in `priv/cmap/` — the decoder dispatches to
22 `Pdf.Reader.CID.Decoder.build_predefined/2` which uses
23 `Pdf.Reader.CID.PredefinedCMap` for byte→CID lookup followed by the
24 same Adobe registry → Unicode resolution as Identity-H/V.
25
26 ## Cache
27
28 Decoders for fonts referenced by indirect ref `{:ref, n, g}` are cached in
29 `Document.cache` under key `{:font_decoder, {n, g}}` for reuse across pages
30 with shared font resources. Inline font dicts (plain maps, no ref) are NOT
31 cached.
32
33 ## Recovery mode (R-2)
34
35 When `doc.recover_mode` is `true` and a font dict fails to resolve or build,
36 `build_decoders_for_resources/2` installs a fallback U+FFFD identity decoder
37 for that font instead of returning `{:error, _}`. The fallback emits
38 `<<0xFFFD::utf8>>` per input byte, which guarantees `String.valid?/1` is
39 `true` on the resulting text. A `{:font_skipped, page_n, font_name, reason}`
40 event is logged to `doc.recovery_log` for each failed font. Fonts that build
41 successfully are NOT affected.
42
43 Spec: PDF 1.7 § 9.6 (font dictionaries), § 9.10 (text content extraction).
