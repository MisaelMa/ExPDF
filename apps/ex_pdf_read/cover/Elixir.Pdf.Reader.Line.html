3 Logical text line reconstructed from individual `TextRun`s.
4
5 Many PDFs (particularly machine-generated ones such as government
6 forms and tax documents) place glyphs individually with the `TJ`
7 operator and per-glyph kerning, producing one `TextRun` per character.
8 Working with that flat run list is awkward — `Line` coalesces those
9 runs into the structure a human reader sees: lines and, within each
10 line, tokens separated by visible whitespace.
11
12 ## Shape
13
14 - `:page` — 1-indexed page number
15 - `:y` — baseline Y of the line (PDF user-space, origin bottom-left)
16 - `:x` — leftmost X of the first token on the line
17 - `:text` — joined text, tokens separated by single spaces
18 - `:tokens` — ordered list of `t:token/0` maps, sorted by X ascending
19
20 Each token carries its own `:x` so callers can detect column layouts
21 (e.g. table rows where every line has tokens at the same X positions).
22
