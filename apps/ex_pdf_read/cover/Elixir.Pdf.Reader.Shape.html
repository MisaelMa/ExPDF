2 @moduledoc """
3 Polymorphic struct describing an "interactive" or actionable element
4 extracted from a PDF — currently link-like elements (URIs, emails,
5 intra-document jumps).
6
7 A shape may come from one of three sources:
8
9 - `:annotation` — a real PDF annotation of subtype `/Link` that the
10 document author placed on the page (PDF 1.7 § 12.5.6.5).
11 - `:inferred` — a URL or email address that appears as plain text in
12 the page content but is not wrapped in a clickable annotation. This
13 is common in government forms (e.g. the SAT CSF prints
14 `http://sat.gob.mx` as text without making it a link). We pattern-
15 match URI and email tokens to surface these to callers.
16 - `:embedded` — a non-text element drawn into the page content
17 (currently raster images via `Do` operators on `/Subtype /Image`
18 XObjects, PDF 1.7 § 8.9). The reader surfaces these so callers
19 can know an image exists at a position even if they can't decode
20 its contents (e.g. a QR code rendered as PNG).
21
22 ## Fields
23
24 - `:type` — one of `:uri | :email | :goto | :launch | :named | :image`
25 - `:page` — 1-indexed page number where the shape lives
26 - `:rect` — `{x1, y1, x2, y2}` user-space bounding box, or `nil` when
27 the source is `:inferred` and the bounding box could not be derived
28 from token positions
29 - `:target` — for `:uri`/`:email`: the URI/address as a string. For
30 `:goto`: a map `%{page: n}`. For `:image`: the indirect ref
31 `{n, g}` of the underlying XObject. For `:launch`/`:named`: see
32 PDF 1.7 § 12.6.4 — currently surfaced as a raw string when known.
33 - `:text` — visible text of the shape (annotation `:contents`, or the
34 matched token text for inferred shapes). `nil` for images.
35 - `:source` — `:annotation`, `:inferred`, or `:embedded`
36 - `:meta` — type-specific extras as a map. For `:image`:
37 `%{format: :png_like | :jpeg, width: w, height: h, byte_size: n}`.
38 Empty for link-like shapes today; future kinds (`:button`,
39 `:form_field`) will populate it.
40
